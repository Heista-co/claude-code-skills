## Hook Subtypes

What stops the scroll. These are the opening beat mechanisms.

- **CURIOSITY_SPIKE**: Exploits the information gap effect. The brain can't rest until it closes an open question — they HAVE to keep watching.
- **HIDDEN_TRUTH_REVEAL**: Activates novelty-seeking behavior. The brain assigns higher value to "hidden" information, making it feel exclusive.
- **UNEXPECTED_FACT_START**: Creates cognitive dissonance. When a fact clashes with existing beliefs, the brain demands resolution.
- **IDENTITY_HOOK**: Triggers self-referential processing. When the viewer hears their identity described, the message becomes personally relevant.
- **TRIBE_CALL_OUT**: Activates in-group recognition. The brain processes "people like me" signals faster than any other content.
- **DIRECT_QUESTION_HOOK**: Forces involuntary mental participation. The brain auto-generates answers to direct questions — it's neurological compulsion.
- **STORY_START**: Hijacks the brain's narrative transport. Once a story begins, the viewer enters the scene and defenses drop.
- **SYMPTOM_STACK**: Casts a self-qualifying net. Stacked symptom fragments make the viewer auto-match before they can scroll.
- **SHOCK_PATTERN_BREAK**: Jolts the brain out of scroll autopilot. Pattern interrupts activate the amygdala — the brain's threat detector — forcing attention.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF_HOOK**: Leverages herd instinct. The brain uses other people's behavior as proof of correctness.
- **AUTHORITY_SIGNAL**: Activates the brain's deference-to-expertise shortcut. When a credible source speaks, critical filters lower.
- **EMOTIONAL_TRIGGER**: Hits the limbic system before the rational brain kicks in. Emotional processing is 5x faster than logical processing.
- **PROMISE_HOOK**: Activates the brain's reward prediction system. A clear promise creates dopamine release in anticipation.
- **CONTRAST_HOOK**: The brain is wired to notice differences. Before/after, old way/new way — contrast makes value instantly visceral.
- **BELIEF_BREAK**: Shatters a mental model. When a long-held belief is challenged, the brain becomes hyper-receptive to the new framing.
- **DISRUPTIVE_STATEMENT**: Says something that contradicts what the viewer expects. Cognitive surprise forces the brain to stop and process.
- **FEATURE_CASCADE**: Overwhelms the rational brain with rapid value signals. Each feature stacks on the last, creating a "too good to ignore" effect.
- **VULNERABLE_CONFESSION**: Triggers empathy and trust circuits. Vulnerability signals authenticity — the brain drops its sales resistance.
- **RESULT_FLASH**: Shows the end state first. The brain's visual system locks onto the result and reverse-engineers desire: "I want that."
- **OPEN_LOOP_STATEMENT**: Creates an incomplete narrative the brain must resolve. Open loops feel like unfinished business.
- **CONTRADICTION_HOOK**: States two things that shouldn't be true at the same time. The brain pauses to reconcile the conflict.
- **PROVOCATION**: Makes a bold, potentially controversial claim. Triggers the brain's debate reflex — engagement follows automatically.
- **CONFLICT_STATEMENT**: Introduces opposing forces or ideas. The brain is drawn to conflict because it signals important information.
- **CHALLENGE_INTRO**: Issues a dare or challenge. The brain's competitive circuits activate — viewers stay to see the outcome.
- **ROLE_SPECIFIC_OPENING**: Addresses a specific professional or personal role. Relevance filtering happens instantly.
- **RHETORICAL_QUESTION**: Poses a question that implies its own answer. The brain processes the implied answer as self-generated insight.
- **DIAGNOSTIC_QUESTION**: Asks the viewer to evaluate themselves. Self-assessment triggers reflection and personal relevance.

## Psychology Missions

The deeper play on the buyer's brain. This is what the ad is really doing psychologically.

- **CURIOSITY_GAP**: Opens a question and resolves it within the video. The brain treats unresolved curiosity like a physical itch — they'll watch to the end just to close the loop.
- **NOVELTY_REWARD**: Something weird, unusual, or unexpected that activates the brain's novelty-seeking reward system. New information triggers dopamine.
- **LOSS_AVERSION**: Losing activates pain centers 2x more than gaining activates pleasure. This ad emphasizes what the viewer LOSES by not acting.
- **EMOTIONAL_SPIKE**: Creates strong emotional arousal (anger, joy, shock, awe) that burns into memory. Strong emotions make the experience more memorable and shareable.
- **IDENTITY_CONFIRMATION**: "You are the type of person who..." — triggers self-referential processing. People buy things that match their self-image.
- **STATUS_ASSERTION**: Elevates the viewer's social standing or relative position. The product becomes a status signal.
- **BELONGING_SIGNAL**: "Join the group that..." — activates the deep human need to be part of something. The product becomes a membership badge.
- **MEANING_MAKING**: Connects the purchase to something bigger than the product. The buyer isn't buying a thing — they're buying purpose, values, or a mission.
- **COMPETENCE_RESTORATION**: Addresses the feeling of "I should be better at this." The product restores their sense of capability — it's about feeling competent again.
- **THREAT_REDUCTION**: Addresses immediate danger or survival concern. Fear to relief is one of the most powerful persuasion sequences in advertising.
- **HOPE_PROJECTION**: Paints a vivid future state the buyer wants to inhabit. The brain struggles to distinguish vividly imagined futures from real experiences.
- **EMPATHY_CONNECTION**: Shared experience creates emotional mirroring and trust. When someone feels truly understood, trust forms instantly.
- **SOCIAL_VALIDATION**: Others' approval or high engagement signals collective approval. The brain uses social validation as a correctness shortcut.
- **BEHAVIOURAL_DISRUPTION**: Breaks autopilot. An unexpected pattern interrupts normal cognitive flow, creating an opening for a new solution.
- **INTRINSIC_MOTIVATION**: Taps into internal drives (mastery, autonomy, purpose) rather than external reward. The buyer wants it because it aligns with who they want to become.
- **CLOSURE_DELIVERY**: Resolves tension created earlier. The brain craves closure — and the CTA becomes the path to getting it.

## Creative Formats

How the ad is built. The production format and visual structure.

- **TALKING_HEAD_BROLL**: Person speaks to camera with frequent cuts to product shots and lifestyle footage. Combines the trust of eye contact with visual proof. The workhorse of UGC ads.
- **TALKING_HEAD_PRODUCT**: Person speaks while holding/showing product throughout. Dual-channel persuasion: voice builds trust while product demo builds desire.
- **TALKING_HEAD_SCREEN**: Person speaking combined with screen recording/UI walkthrough. Shows the product in action while a human guides the narrative.
- **TALKING_HEAD_SOLO**: Pure monologue — person speaks directly to camera with no cutaways. Maximum authenticity signal. The brain reads this as "real person, real opinion."
- **FOUNDER_TO_CAMERA**: Brand founder/CEO speaking directly to camera. Carries extra authority because the brain processes "person who built this" as the ultimate expert.
- **VOICEOVER_BROLL**: Voiceover narrates over product shots and lifestyle footage. Lets the visuals do emotional work while the voice guides the rational narrative.
- **VOICEOVER_SCREEN**: Voiceover narrates over screen recording/app walkthrough. Keeps focus entirely on the product without the distraction of a face.
- **PRODUCT_DEMO**: Close-up, hands-on footage showing product in use. "Seeing is believing" — the brain trusts demonstrated proof over stated claims.
- **PRODUCT_HERO**: High-production product shots — studio lighting, beauty angles, slow motion. The brain associates beautiful presentation with product quality.
- **BEFORE_AFTER**: Structured around visible transformation — split screen or sequential. The most powerful proof format in D2C.
- **STREET_INTERVIEW**: Host asks strangers in public about a product. Activates social proof and authenticity simultaneously.
- **BRAND_FILM**: Cinematic narrative-driven content with agency-level craft. The brain enters narrative mode and drops its ad resistance entirely.
- **SKIT_NARRATIVE**: Scripted, acted content with characters and storyline. Entertainment lowers ad resistance.
- **MASHUP_COMPILATION**: Multiple clips/testimonials stitched together into montage. Creates a "greatest hits" effect.
- **MOTION_GRAPHICS**: Primarily text, animation, or graphic-driven content. Great for explaining complex concepts visually.
- **SLIDESHOW_STATICS**: Sequence of static images with transitions and music. Low-cost, high-volume format.
- **GREEN_SCREEN**: Person with virtual background. Native to TikTok culture.
- **REACTION_COMMENTARY**: Creator reacting to another piece of content. Leverages existing cultural moments.
- **GRWM_ROUTINE**: Get Ready With Me or daily routine format. Product naturally integrated into real life.
- **INTERVIEW_PODCAST**: Conversation or interview format. Long-form authority building.
- **TEXT_STORY**: Narrative told through text on screen — fake text conversations, DM exchanges. Creates intimacy.
- **SCREEN_SCROLL**: Faceless recording of scrolling through social proof. Visual evidence of overwhelming positive signals.
- **ASMR_SENSORY**: Sensory-focused — satisfying sounds, textures, close-ups. Activates the brain's sensory pleasure circuits.
- **CAROUSEL_VIDEO**: Sequence of designed cards/slides with branded frames.
- **SPLIT_SCREEN**: Deliberate side-by-side layout. Forces comparison.
- **UNBOXING**: Opening and experiencing product for the first time. Captures anticipation and novelty.
- **LIFESTYLE**: Product embedded in desirable lifestyle context. The brain sees a life it wants. The product becomes a gateway.

## Marketing Angles

The persuasion strategy. What approach the ad takes to convince.

- **ASPIRATIONAL_IDENTITY**: Sells lifestyle, identity, aspiration — "this is who you become." Minimal product specs, mood-driven. The brain is motivated by identity gaps.
- **BEHIND_THE_SCENES**: Shows how product is made/sourced/designed. Transparency builds trust because the brain interprets openness as "nothing to hide."
- **COMMUNITY_MOVEMENT**: Emphasizes belonging to a group/movement. The brain's tribal instinct makes people want to join something bigger.
- **COMPARISON_VS**: Positions product against competitors or the old way. Controls the comparison frame so the product always wins.
- **EDUCATION_VALUE**: Leads with genuinely useful teaching before product mention. The brain rewards learning with dopamine and associates the teacher with expertise.
- **EXPERT_AUTHORITY**: Credentialed expert endorsing. The brain's authority bias means "an expert said so" is processed as near-fact.
- **GUARANTEE_RISK_REVERSAL**: Leads with removing purchase risk. The brain's loss aversion makes risk the biggest barrier — removing risk removes the barrier.
- **HOW_TO_TUTORIAL**: Practical instruction showing how to use product. Makes the buyer mentally rehearse using it — creating ownership before purchase.
- **HUMOR_ENTERTAINMENT**: Leads with comedy and entertainment value. Laughter triggers endorphins — the brain links "this made me feel good" with the product.
- **INGREDIENT_SCIENCE**: Leads with ingredients, formulation, clinical data. Satisfies the rational brain's need for "why does this work?"
- **MYTH_BUSTING**: Challenges conventional wisdom. Creates an "aha moment" that reframes understanding.
- **OBJECTION_HANDLING**: Directly addresses common objections preventing purchase. Names the objection and dismantles it.
- **OFFER_URGENCY**: Leads with deal/discount/time-limited promotion. The brain processes scarcity as threat — triggers faster, more emotional decisions.
- **ORIGIN_STORY**: Tells WHY brand/product exists. Origin stories create emotional investment.
- **PROBLEM_AWARENESS**: Identifies a problem the audience may not realize they have. Once the brain registers a problem, it immediately wants a solution.
- **PROBLEM_SOLUTION**: Problem-agitate-solution arc with product as hero. Pain deeply processed, then relief when the solution appears — contrast amplifies value.
- **PRODUCT_LAUNCH**: Announces something new. Novelty bias makes "new" inherently interesting.
- **SEASONAL_TRIGGER**: Anchored to specific time/season/event. Makes the decision feel timely.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF_RESULTS**: Evidence from customers — testimonials, reviews, before/afters. Others' success processed as predictive of own results.
- **TREND_CULTURE**: Rides current trend/meme/cultural moment. Makes the product feel like the smart, modern choice.
- **VALUE_STACK**: Stacks multiple benefits and bonuses. Each additional value point makes the offer feel more unfair (in the buyer's favor).

## Video Types

Content category — what kind of video this is.

- **TUTORIAL_HOW_TO**: Step-by-step instruction. Positions the brand as a helpful expert.
- **PRODUCT_DEMO**: Features and benefits shown in use. The brain trusts what it can see working.
- **PROBLEM_SOLUTION**: Clear problem-agitate-solution structure. The product is the hero.
- **EXPERT_INSIGHT**: Expert commentary or professional analysis. Credentialed knowledge accepted more readily.
- **UGC_TESTIMONIAL**: Personal product experience narrative. Peer voice is 3x more trusted than brand voice.
- **PRODUCT_COMPARISON**: Side-by-side comparison. Controls the evaluation frame.
- **RESULTS_SHOWCASE**: Focused on outcomes and proof. Screenshots, metrics, visual evidence.
- **ROUTINE_INTEGRATION**: Product naturally integrated into daily routine. Feels like a recommendation, not an ad.
- **FOUNDER_ORIGIN**: The founding story. Creates emotional investment in the person behind the product.
- **STORY_EXPERIENCE**: Personal narrative about a real experience. Authentic and relatable.
- **CHALLENGE_EXPERIMENT**: "I tried X for 30 days." Curiosity-driven format.
- **SKIT_ROLEPLAY**: Scripted scene that entertains while delivering the message.
- **TREND_NATIVE**: Uses trending format/audio/meme. Feels native to the platform.
- **DAY_IN_LIFE**: Full day structure with product as ambient presence. Aspirational lifestyle.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF_STACK**: Multiple people's results compiled together. Quantity overwhelms skepticism.
- **OBJECTION_BUSTER**: Directly addresses product-specific doubts. Removes barriers.
- **LISTICLE**: Numbered list of reasons, mistakes, or information. Structured and scannable.
- **INGREDIENT_SCIENCE**: Specific ingredients with scientific explanation. Mechanism-level trust.

## Algorithm Intents

Structural architecture — how the ad is engineered to perform on the platform.

- **RETENTION_LOOP_ENGINE**: Structure encourages rewatching or loops back to start. High retention signals quality to algorithms.
- **CURIOSITY_GAP_COMPRESSION**: Opens question early, resolves within the video. The brain can't leave until the gap closes.
- **CONTRADICTORY_PATTERN_INTERRUPT**: Opens with direct contradiction of what viewer believes. Forces engagement.
- **EMOTIONAL_ARC_ESCALATION**: Emotions escalate throughout. Builds intensity systematically.
- **VALUE_STACK_ACCELERATION**: Rapid-fires benefits without deep explanation. Creates "too much value to ignore" compound effect.
- **MICRO_STORY_PAYOFF_ENGINE**: Small story arc with quick payoff. Tiny narrative with resolution — satisfying and shareable.
- **DISCOVERY_EDUCATION_DRIVER**: Teaches broadly at educational pace. The brain rewards learning.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF_ACCELERATOR**: Multiple customer voices/results stacked. Quantity builds conviction.
- **IDENTITY_RESOLUTION_HOOK**: "You are the type of person who..." — self-referential positioning.
- **COMMENT_BAIT_INTERACTION_DRIVER**: Structured to provoke comments. More engagement = more distribution.
- **SHAREABILITY_SOCIAL_REPLICATION**: Designed for viewers to share or replicate. Viral mechanics built in.
- **CTA_OPTIMIZED_CONVERSION**: Entire ad architected around driving action. CTA woven throughout.
- **PROBLEM_AGITATE_SOLVE**: Pain, amplify pain, product as relief. Classic copywriting formula.
- **MECHANISM_REVEAL**: Reveals HOW the product works at a deeper level. Creates educated buyers.
- **AUTHORITY_CREDIBILITY_TRANSFER**: Expert/authority lends credibility to the product.
- **RISK_REVERSAL_CLOSE**: Conversion lever is guarantee/free trial. Removes the biggest barrier.
- **SCARCITY_URGENCY_ENGINE**: Time/stock pressure creates urgency. The brain treats scarcity as threat.
- **OFFER_STACK_OVERWHELM**: Stacking deal components until the offer feels too good to refuse.
- **TRANSFORMATION_ARC_ENGINE**: Journey from current state to desired state. The product is the vehicle.
- **COMMUNITY_BELONGING_ENGINE**: Sells membership, not just a product. Tribal motivation.

## Behavioral Roles

The speaker's position — what role they play relative to the viewer.

- **DEMAND_GENERATION_PRIME**: Surfaces the problem/desire without offering a specific solution. Plants the seed.
- **SELL_PRODUCT_DIRECT**: Pure direct-response pitch. Straight to the point.
- **SOFT_SELL_VALUE_FRAME**: Strategic value positioning with implied CTA. The buyer feels like they discovered the product.
- **TEACH_EXPLAIN**: Teaching-focused, leads with instruction. Trust transfers from the lesson to the product.
- **DEMONSTRATE_METHOD**: Shows how it works. Hands-on proof of the method or process.
- **PEER_RECOMMEND**: "You NEED this" — friend recommending with casual enthusiasm. Peer voice bypasses the ad filter.
- **ENTERTAIN_TO_SELL**: Entertainment-led with product woven in. Resistance drops.
- **SOLVE_YOUR_PROBLEM**: Acknowledges pain AND offers product as relief. Empathetic pitch.
- **CHALLENGE_REFRAME**: "Stop doing X" — confrontational, then sells the alternative.
- **CURATE_RECOMMEND**: "I tested 10 of these, this one wins" — curator/tester role.
- **CONFESS_DISCOVER**: "I wasn't expecting this" — discovery confession. Authentic surprise builds trust.

## Beat Subtypes

Each structural category (OPENING, CONTEXT, TENSION, DELIVERY, VALIDATION, SHIFT, CLOSE) contains specific subtypes. The key ones:

### OPENING Subtypes
See [Hook Subtypes](#hook-subtypes) above — they're the same dimension.

### CONTEXT Subtypes
- **OBJECT_INTRO**: Introduces the product as a physical object. Gives the brain a concrete focal point.
- **SCENE_SET**: Establishes the environment, situation, or lifestyle context.
- **BACKSTORY**: Provides personal or brand history that contextualizes what follows.
- **PROBLEM_FRAME**: Names the specific problem that creates the need.

### DELIVERY Subtypes
- **FEATURE_BREAKDOWN**: Converts abstract value into concrete understanding. Specific features explained.
- **FEATURE_CASCADE**: Stacks features rapidly. Accumulation effect builds perceived value.
- **BENEFIT_REVEAL**: Translates features into outcomes the buyer cares about.
- **MECHANISM_EXPLAIN**: Reveals how or why the product works at a deeper level.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF_DROP**: Introduces external validation — reviews, results, testimonials.
- **PRICE_VALUE_FRAME**: Positions the price as justified or advantageous.

### SHIFT Subtypes
- **EASY_WAY_REVEAL**: Reveals a simpler path than expected. Effort discounting makes simplicity persuasive.
- **PERSPECTIVE_FLIP**: Reframes the viewer's understanding. What they thought was X is actually Y.
- **EMOTIONAL_PIVOT**: Shifts the emotional register — from tension to relief, from logic to feeling.

### CLOSE Subtypes
- **LESSON_CLOSE**: Ends with a compressed takeaway. Recency effect makes the last thing stated most memorable.
- **CTA_DIRECT**: Explicit call to action — "click the link," "shop now."
- **SCARCITY_CTA**: CTA combined with urgency or limited availability.
- **IDENTITY_CTA**: CTA framed as identity confirmation — "if you're serious about X, this is for you."
- **LOOP_CLOSE**: Ends by referencing the opening, creating a satisfying loop.

## Static Ad Taxonomy

For image ad decodes (when available).

### Formats
- **PRODUCT_HERO_OFFER**: Product centered with discount/offer text. Direct conversion play.
- **PRODUCT_HERO_BENEFIT**: Product centered with benefit headline.
- **LIFESTYLE_BENEFIT**: Person in context with benefit text. Dual persuasion.
- **UGC_STYLE**: User-generated content aesthetic. Looks real, not branded. 3x more trusted.
- **TESTIMONIAL_PROOF**: Review/quote with product image. Social proof in visual form.
- **STAT_IMPACT**: Big numbers/stats as focal point. Specific numbers feel more credible.
- **URGENCY_OFFER**: Time-limited deal emphasis. Scarcity triggers loss aversion.
- **COMPARISON_GRID**: Before/after or multi-option comparison.

### Scroll-Stop Mechanisms
- **BOLD_COLOR_CONTRAST**: High-contrast colors that pop against the feed. Hardwired to notice contrast.
- **HUMAN_FACE**: The brain has dedicated neural hardware for face detection — nothing grabs attention faster.
- **PATTERN_INTERRUPT**: Something visually unexpected that breaks the scroll.
- **BEFORE_AFTER**: Visual transformation. Change detection is automatic.
- **LARGE_NUMBER**: Numbers are processed faster than text — visual shortcuts to credibility.

### Persuasion Techniques
- **ANCHORING**: First price becomes the reference point. Makes the sale price feel like a steal.
- **SCARCITY**: "Only 50 left" — the brain values scarce things more.
- **SOCIAL_PROOF**: Reviews, testimonials, "10,000+ sold." The crowd as quality shortcut.
- **LOSS_AVERSION**: Framed as what you'll LOSE, not gain. Losses hurt 2x more.
- **RISK_REVERSAL**: Guarantee or money-back. Eliminates the biggest barrier to purchase.
- **VALUE_STACKING**: Piling benefits until perceived value vastly exceeds the price.
- **SPECIFICITY**: "97.3% of users saw results in 14 days." Precise numbers feel more credible.
