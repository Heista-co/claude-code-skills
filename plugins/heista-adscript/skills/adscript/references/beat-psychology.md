# Beat Psychology Reference

Psychology-driven descriptions for all beat subtypes in the formula system. Use this when writing scripts without corpus data, or when you need to understand what a specific subtype mechanism does to the viewer.

## OPENING Subtypes

### CURIOSITY_SPIKE
Exploits the information gap effect. The brain can't rest until it closes an open question, so the viewer cannot scroll away. Write an incomplete reveal or a teasing claim that demands the next beat.

### OPEN_LOOP_STATEMENT
Triggers the Zeigarnik effect. Incomplete thoughts create psychological tension that only watching can resolve. Start a thought, leave it hanging.

### HIDDEN_TRUTH_REVEAL
Activates novelty-seeking behavior. The brain assigns higher value to "hidden" information. Frame the product or insight as something most people don't know.

### UNEXPECTED_FACT_START
Creates cognitive dissonance. When a fact clashes with beliefs, the brain demands resolution. Lead with a stat or claim that surprises.

### CONTRADICTION_HOOK
Triggers a prediction error. When something sounds wrong, the brain's threat-detection fires. Say something that contradicts conventional wisdom.

### PROVOCATION
Activates the amygdala through emotional charge. Strong reactions override the impulse to scroll. Make a bold, provocative claim.

### IDENTITY_HOOK
Triggers self-referential processing. When the viewer hears their identity described, the message feels instantly personal. Call out who they are.

### DIRECT_QUESTION_HOOK
Forces involuntary mental participation. The brain auto-generates answers to direct questions, ending passive scrolling. Ask something specific.

### STORY_START
Activates narrative transportation. Once a story begins, the brain needs resolution. Start a personal or brand story mid-action.

### DISCOVERY_MOMENT
Exploits the "eureka" response. Frame the product as something you just found or figured out. Personal discovery is more credible than advertising.

## CONTEXT Subtypes

### PROBLEM_SETUP
Names the pain clearly. The viewer recognizes their frustration and leans in. Be specific about the problem, not generic.

### OBJECT_INTRO
Introduces the product as a physical thing. Show it, name it, place it in the viewer's world. First impression matters.

### SCENE_SETTING
Establishes the world of the ad. Location, lifestyle, moment. Makes the viewer picture themselves in the scenario.

### CREDIBILITY_FRAME
Establishes why the speaker has authority. Title, experience, credentials. Done quickly, not bragged about.

## TENSION Subtypes

### PAIN_AMPLIFIER
Makes the problem feel worse. Stacks consequences, shows what happens if nothing changes. Increases desire for the solution.

### URGENCY_BUILDER
Creates time pressure. Scarcity, limited offers, seasonal relevance. Makes the viewer feel they need to act now.

### DESIRE_ESCALATION
Builds wanting through imagery and imagination. Paints the after picture. Makes the viewer feel what it would be like to have the product.

### FRICTION_REVEAL
Shows the gap between where they are and where they want to be. The gap creates tension that only the product resolves.

## DELIVERY Subtypes

### FEATURE_CASCADE
Stacks features at increasing speed. Each feature adds value on top of the last. Builds momentum toward "how is this not enough?"

### BENEFIT_BRIDGE
Translates features into outcomes. Not "25g protein" but "you stay full until lunch without snacking." Every feature earns a benefit.

### MECHANISM_EXPLANATION
Shows HOW it works, not just WHAT it does. The "secret ingredient" moment. Makes the product feel scientific or inevitable.

### SOCIAL_PROOF_DROP
Drops proof mid-delivery. A number, a testimonial snippet, a result. Not a full case study, just a credibility spike.

### VALUE_STACK
Piles everything the buyer gets. Features + bonuses + guarantees. Makes the price feel irrational relative to the value.

## VALIDATION Subtypes

### PROOF_POINT
A single, specific piece of evidence. One stat, one result, one testimonial. Crisp and undeniable.

### BEFORE_AFTER
Shows transformation. The contrast between with and without the product. Visual or verbal.

### TESTIMONIAL_CLIP
Someone else saying it works. More credible than the brand saying it. Keep it short and specific.

### RESULT_SHOWCASE
Displays outcomes. Numbers, visuals, demonstrations. Lets the results speak.

## SHIFT Subtypes

### NARRATIVE_PIVOT
Changes the direction of the story. From problem to solution. From features to emotion. From "them" to "you." The turn that reframes everything.

### CONTRAST_BRIDGE
Before/after in miniature. "I used to [old way], now I [new way]." Creates a clear line between the viewer's current state and the promised state.

## CLOSE Subtypes

### SOFT_CTA
Advice, not advertising. "Try it." "Give it a shot." "See for yourself." Feels like a friend's recommendation, not a sales pitch.

### DIRECT_CTA
Clear instruction. "Click below." "Order now." "Link in bio." No ambiguity about what to do next.

### TAGLINE_CLOSE
Ends with the brand's identity line. Memorable, repeatable, positions the product in one phrase.

### URGENCY_CTA
CTA with a deadline or scarcity element. "Sale ends tonight." "Only 200 left." Adds consequence to inaction.

### REFRAME_CLOSE
Final perspective shift. Makes the viewer see the product differently right before the decision moment. "It's not a robe. It's your morning ritual."
